The Harvometer

The Challenge

Chancellor Harvey Perlman wants 30,000 students at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln by 2020. The challenge? Since 1967, the university has never been closer to the goal than 25,000 students. Since the 1960s, enrollment has hovered roughly between 22,000 and 24,000 students.

Things are Getting Better

Higher Graduation Rates

Since 1990s, the university's graduation rate has been steadily rising. However, its six-year graduation rate is dead last in the Big 10.

Retention Rates are Up

The university's first-year retention rate is up from a low of 72 percent in 1993 to nearly 84 percent in 2012.
